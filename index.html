Overview
Description: The goal of this project was to redesign the Dream Center website with a focus on the information architecture improvements and provide more transparency of the organization.

Client: The Dream Center is a non-profit organization based in Los Angeles, California. Its mission is to offer hope and support to people facing challenges such as homelessness, poverty, addiction, and lack of education.

My Role: User Research, Information Architecture, Competitive Analysis, Interaction Design, Prototyping.

Tools: Figma, Miro, Trello, etc.

Time Frame: 3 Weeks.

User ResearchDefinitionStep 1: User ResearchWe kicked off our project with User Research, in order to gain a clear understanding of Dream Center’s users. Our team discovered that the website caters to three distinct user groups, but our primary focus was on volunteers.
We wanted to create a realistic proto-persona to get an idea of our targeted users. We hypothesized that our users would have a strong interest in volunteering. Are frustrated with aligning organizations to personal interests, a lack of responses from non-profits, and unsure of fund allocation.
Proto PersonaConducted a Qualitative & Quantitative ResearchConducted google form survey & 5 in-person interviews with volunteers to understand motivations, frustrations, and preferences. Identified key insights such as the need for clear mission communication and transparent impact reporting.
5 Interviews44
Survey Responses
We each conducted 1 semi-structured interview to understand user’s expectations and pain points of DC website. 44 individuals unanimously responded to a survey we created using Google Forms. The aim was to understand individuals’ pains and preferences.
Key Takeaway

People decide to volunteer for nonprofits that:

Are aligned with personal beliefs.

Include a clear description of goals and mission.
